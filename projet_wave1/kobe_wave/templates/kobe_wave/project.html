About the project

Wave is a project conceived by Federico Biggio and developed in partnership with students from CESR. It explores the visual, editorial, and cultural legacy of Wired magazine through an approach blending design, digital humanities, and data analysis. The goal is to create an experimental space where one can observe, manipulate, and interpret the elements that have shaped the magazine's identity, while offering a contemporary reinterpretation of its imagery.

Wave is a project led by Federico Biggio, a lecturer and researcher in information and communication sciences at the University of Tours. Its objective is to offer a critical perspective on culture, information visualization, and research in the age of AI. This project is also supported by two students from the Center for Advanced Renaissance Studies. First, Kossi Zangbe, with a background in anthropology, is interested in cultural dynamics and social representations, with a particular interest in technological imaginaries and figures of media power. Finally, Orphée Bonnardeau, trained in literature, history, arts, and culture, is comfortable with graphic design tools and critical approaches to visual storytelling. Together, the two students were able to develop the project from start to finish with the help of Federico Biggio and their professors.

MR.BIGGIO

django

For this project, we chose to use Django, a Python web framework known for its robustness and ease of implementation. The two team members familiarized themselves with this tool, exploring its features before gradually and rigorously integrating it into the project. Django was incorporated according to recommended best practices, notably by adhering to the MVT (Model-View-Template) architecture, which clearly structures the code. It allowed us to manage the database efficiently and optimally, thanks to its ORM (Object-Relational Mapping), which facilitates interactions with the data without requiring complex SQL queries. Managing migrations, models, and views was greatly simplified, making the project more maintainable and scalable.
